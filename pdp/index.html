Where did I come from?

I come from a background that combines traditional and digital creativity. I have experience in painting, especially oil, where I focus on figurative work with classical techniques but also a contemporary touch. I also explore tattooing as a way to maintain artistic discipline and expand my skills. My greatest influences have always been classical painters, Pre-Raphaelites, cinema, music, and books. In addition, I have worked in design which gave me tools for visual communication and organization. These have shaped how I approach both art and creative processes.

What do I want to improve the coming weeks?

I deeply respect classical painting, and oil painting remains one of my great passions, as it has trained me in a technique that demands patience, precision, and detail. However, I am here because I want to open my mind to new forms of creation and explore other processes. I am interested in approaching faster, rougher, more intuitive, imperfect and colourful dynamics, where it is not essential to start from a well-defined base drawing, but simply to let myself be carried away by the act of creating. I have begun to experiment with oil pastels, markers and airbrushes. Of course, I want to continue to delve deeper into oil painting and realism, but at the same time I want to broaden my practice, discover freer languages and, perhaps, find ways to combine both dimensions.

I also want to explore more about the international art world, expanding my references and understanding different contemporary practices. Additionally, I want to work on my mindset, being less self-critical and more open to experimentation, while learning how to balance art, work, and personal growth.

How will I achieve this?

  • Dedicate fixed daily time slots to create, and sketching ideas for my series.

  • Research and analyze small-scale figurative painters and how they handle intimacy and symbolism.

  • Visit at least one exhibition in the coming weeks and reflect on how it connects to my own practice.

  • Use sketchbooks as a tool for experimentation without pressure—drawing, writing, and exploring compositions freely.

  • Reflection exercises to regulate self-criticism and stay connected to my goals.

Also, when I'm stuck, I use a method of asking myself questions to get things moving again and get back to creating. I'll share them with you :)

• What emotion do I want to convey with this painting? • Do I want to tell a story, express something personal, or simply experiment with technique? • If I had to choose one word to sum up the idea behind the painting, what would it be? • Is there a recent experience or recurring thought that I want to capture? • Do I prefer to work with human figures, objects, landscapes, or something more abstract? • Which format inspires me most: vertical, horizontal, or square? • Do I want a minimalist approach or one full of details? • Where do I want the focus of the work to be? • What feeling do I want to create: calm, tension, movement, silence...? • Do I want to experiment with something new or reinforce my classic style? • What kind of light appeals to me for this painting: dramatic, soft, cold, warm? • Am I interested in working with a limited palette or with many colours? • Do I want to use only oil or integrate other techniques such as charcoal, ink or airbrush?
